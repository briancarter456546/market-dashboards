2. Bank reserve plumbing under stress at quarter-end

UNDER-COVERED TREND: The overnight reverse repo facility (ON RRP) usage dropped to 8B, the lowest since March 2021, indicating money market fund cash is migrating to higher-yielding T-bills and private repo.

WHY IGNORED: Reserve plumbing is too technical for mainstream coverage; only the Fed's own H.4.1 report and repo market specialists track this daily.

HISTORICAL TRAJECTORY: ON RRP peaked at .55T in December 2022. The drain has been steady but accelerated in Q1 2026 as T-bill supply increased from Treasury refunding. The mechanism works via competitive rates: when T-bill yields exceed the ON RRP award rate (currently 5.30%), MMFs shift allocation to bills and private repo, reducing the Fed's liability side. This drains reserves from the banking system as deposits flow to MMFs that no longer park at the Fed.

SYSTEMIC IMPLICATIONS: As ON RRP approaches zero, the system loses its liquidity buffer. Historically, the last 00B of ON RRP coincides with increased SOFR volatility and repo rate spikes at month/quarter-end. The mechanism: banks must then fund positions through the interbank market rather than drawing from the Fed's facility, which concentrates counterparty risk.

EARLY-WARNING SIGNAL: Watch SOFR 99th percentile spread vs EFFR. If spread exceeds 15bps outside quarter-end, the plumbing is strained. Buy short-duration treasuries (SHV) as a safe haven. Sell HYG if SOFR volatility persists.

Falsification: if the Fed adjusts the ON RRP rate or standing repo facility counteracts, the signal weakens.
